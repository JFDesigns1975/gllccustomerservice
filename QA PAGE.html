Q:

How often and when should I water my lawn?

A:

Daily watering for 20 to 30 minutes in each zone during early morning is highly recommended. Newly seeded areas must be watered daily for germination to occur.

Q:

How long should I wait to allow my animals or children to enter the lawn after it has been treated?

A:

After a liquid weed & feed application, we recommended waiting at least 2 hours until the product has time to dry. As far as granular applications are concerned, use the same guidelines as if you were treating the lawn yourself.

Q:

How does the service call policy work and what services are guaranteed?

A:

Treatments that are applied 1 time for specific reasons, such as grub control or fungicide are guaranteed for 30 days. Treatments that are part of any program with 4 or more scheduled applications are backed by free service calls. Treatments in the program are guaranteed up to 21 days and can be reapplied at no cost to the customer within that 21 day window. If a service call is requested after day 21, the next chargeable application will be scheduled and completed as soon as possible.

Q:

How can I help to insure my lawn stays healthy and beautiful?

A:

There are many factors involved when establishing and maintaining a thick and healthy lawn. Weather, types of treatments, hydration, and most importantly communication. Things like disease and insects can have an extreme negative impact on your lawn. Furthermore, the amount of time that lasting damage can occur is a matter of days or weeks. Letting us know that there is turf loss as soon as you see an issue an save time, effort, and money. Once turf has died fertilization will have no effect and replanting and repairing will be the only option. Newly seeded areas must be watered daily to establish new turf, which can take up to 4 weeks to occur. When seeding yourself, please contact our office and mark the areas of new seed. This will help avoid unwanted products from being applied to these areas, which could have a negative affect on the germination of the seed. Additional treatments such as lime, insecticide, or fungicide may be required in order to maintain the beauty of your lawn. Other larger plants can also factor into the health of your grass. In some cases large mature trees can inhibit the growth of grass since they compete for the very fertilizer we apply to your yard. Great Lakes Landcare tailors our programs for each individual situation to best improve the look and health of your lawn. Without proper communication and maintenance from our customers the services we provide may not be enough to give you the thick dream lawn you are looking to establish.
